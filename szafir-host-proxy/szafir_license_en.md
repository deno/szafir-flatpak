1. Under legal regulations:
    a. An electronic signature or electronic seal verified by means of a certificate shall have legal effect, if it has been put within the validity period of the certificate.
    b. An electronic signature or electronic seal put during the period of suspension of the certificate used for its verification has no legal effect, however, when the suspension of the certificate is revoked, the electronic signature or electronic seal verified with this certificate put during the suspension period shall become effective, once the suspension has been lifted.
2. In the light of the above, if the verification of an electronic signature or electronic seal concerns a certificate, the validity period of which has already expired at the time of the verification process, the result of the verification of the electronic signature or electronic seal is undefined, unless the verification is possible based on archived data confirming the status of the certificate.
    The verification result based on archived data may be:
    a. Positive – if the verification of the electronic signature or electronic seal is performed for the past period specified by the Software user and the certificate status information shows that the certificate, for the period, for which the verification is performed, was valid.
    b. Undefined – if the verification of the electronic signature or electronic seal is performed for the past period specified by the Software user and the certificate status information shows that the certificate was suspended during the period, for which the verification is performed, and the beginning of the suspension period is earlier than the indicated past date, as of which verification is performed, and the certificate has not been suspended.
    c. Negative – if the verification of the electronic signature or electronic seal is performed for the past period specified by the Software user and the certificate status information shows that the certificate was invalid at the time, for which the verification has been performed.
3. In the light of the above, if the verification of an electronic signature or electronic seal concerns a certificate, the validity period of which has not yet expired at the time of the verification process, the result of the verification of the electronic signature or electronic seal is:
    a. Positive – if the verification of the electronic signature or electronic seal:
        i. Is performed for the current period and the certificate is valid and not suspended,
        ii. Is performed for the past time specified by the Software user, and the certificate status information shows that:
            • The certificate is currently valid.
            • The certificate is invalid at the current time, but the beginning of the invalidity period is later than the specified past date, for which the verification is performed,
            • The certificate is currently suspended, but the beginning of the suspension period is later than the specified past date, for which verification is performed,
    b. Negative if the verification of the electronic signature or electronic seal:
        i. It is performed for the current period and the certificate is invalid,
        ii. Is performed for the past time specified by the Software user, and the certificate status information shows that the certificate is invalid for the current time and the beginning of the invalidity period is not later than the specified past date, for which the verification is performed,
    c. Undefined, where verification of an electronic signature or electronic seal:
        i. Is performed for the current period and the certificate is suspended,
        ii. Is performed for the past time specified by the Software user, and the certificate status information shows that the certificate is suspended at the current time and the beginning of the suspension period is earlier than the specified past date, for which the verification is performed,
        iii. The software did not obtain information about the certificate status.
